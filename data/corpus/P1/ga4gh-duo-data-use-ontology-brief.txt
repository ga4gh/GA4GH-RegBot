What is DUO?

The Data Use Ontology (DUO) is a standardised vocabulary of terms describing permissions and restrictions on human-subjects data. It enables automated matching between researcher requests and dataset consent limitations, supporting responsible sharing at scale.

Permission terms (examples)

- GRU (General Research Use) — research use unrelated to specific disease or population.
- HMB (Health/Medical/Biomedical Research) — research on health, medical care, or biomedical topics.
- DS (Disease-specific) — modifier requiring research on specified disease (e.g. DS:MONDO:0005148 for type 2 diabetes).

Restriction terms (examples)

- NPU (No commercial use) — prohibits use primarily intended for commercial advantage.
- NCU (No copyright restriction) — publication moratorium or copyright limits may apply per dataset policy.
- GSO (Geographic location) — research limited to specific countries or regions.
- IRB (Ethics approval required) — applicant must demonstrate ethics committee approval.
- PUB (Publication required) — results must be published or made available.
- COL (Collaboration required) — collaboration with data generator required.

How DUO links to consent and GDPR

Human-readable consent forms should express the same permissions/restrictions that are encoded as DUO tags on datasets (see MRCG). DACs verify applicant proposals against DUO. Under GDPR, DUO supports transparency and purpose limitation by documenting authorised use categories.

Cross-border workflow

1. Dataset deposited with DUO tags reflecting participant consent.
2. Researcher submits access request with proposed use.
3. DAC checks DUO compatibility, ethics approval, and transfer compliance.
4. Approved access recorded; violations trigger audit or revocation.

RegBot review prompts

- Are DUO tags attached or inferable from consent / DUL language?
- Does proposed use violate NPU, GSO, or disease-specific modifiers?
- Is IRB/ethics approval required by DUO and satisfied?
- For federated analysis across countries, do geographic restrictions (GSO) block any processing location?

Summary

Genomic research is inherently international. EU-based controllers and processors frequently transfer personal data (including genetic and health data) to collaborators, cloud providers, and repositories outside the European Economic Area (EEA). Chapter V of the GDPR governs such transfers.

When transfers are restricted

Article 44: any transfer of personal data to a third country or international organisation may take place only if the conditions of Chapter V are complied with — adequacy decision, appropriate safeguards, or derogations.

Special category data (Article 9): genetic and health data require an Article 9 lawful basis in addition to transfer compliance. Consent, substantial public interest with Member State law, or scientific research with safeguards are common research contexts.

Transfer mechanisms used in genomic research

1. Adequacy decisions — Commission finds third country ensures adequate protection (e.g. certain territories; list evolves).

2. Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — EU Commission-approved clauses between exporter and importer; post-Schrems II requires Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) documenting supplementary measures if local law may impede protection.

3. Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) — for multinational research institutions with internal transfers.

4. Explicit consent — Article 49(1)(a) derogation: data subject explicitly consents after being informed of transfer risks; generally not preferred as primary basis for systematic research transfers but may apply in limited cases.

5. Public interest / research derogations — Article 49(1)(d) or (g) in non-repetitive transfers; Member State law may authorise transfers for important public interest or legal claims.

Cloud and sequencing vendors

Genomic pipelines often use US or global cloud regions. Controllers must identify subprocessors, document SCCs or other mechanisms, and assess whether foreign surveillance laws require encryption, pseudonymisation, or EU-only processing as supplementary measures.

RegBot review prompts

- Is the destination country identified and covered by adequacy, SCCs, or another Chapter V tool?
- Has a Transfer Impact Assessment been completed for US or non-adequate destinations?
- Does participant consent / ethics approval describe international sharing and recipient categories?
- Are onward transfers (subprocessors, federated analysis) covered in DPA/MTA chain?

Relevant provisions: Articles 44–49, Recital 112, EDPB Recommendations 01/2020 on supplementary measures.

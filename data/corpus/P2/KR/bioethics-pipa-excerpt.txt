Bioethics and Safety Act — human subject research and genetic data

Regulates human subject research, embryo/fetal research limits, and handling of human-derived materials including genetic information. Research protocols typically require institutional review board (IRB) approval.

Collection and use of human tissues, genetic information, and personal data for research must align with informed consent and IRB conditions. Broad or future-use consent is permitted subject to IRB oversight and statutory requirements.

Provision of human-derived materials or genetic information to third parties, including foreign institutions, generally requires consent scope covering such transfer, IRB approval, and compliance with export/security rules where applicable.

National programmes (NBK, CODA, K-BDS, BIKO) operate under ministry oversight with standardised access agreements for domestic and international researchers.

Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) — cross-border transfer

Personal information controllers must obtain data subject consent for cross-border transfer unless an exception applies, and inform subjects of recipient, purpose, items transferred, and retention period.

Sensitive information (including health and potentially genetic data) requires separate consent for collection and use unless another basis exists. Pseudonymisation and technical safeguards are expected for large-scale genomic databases.

Data Protection Commission (PIPC) guidance emphasises accountability, impact assessment for high-risk processing, and documented contracts with overseas processors.

Framework Act on AI (2025/2026) — emerging relevance

High-risk AI using health or biometric data may face additional transparency and risk-management duties; genomic analysis pipelines should be reviewed for alignment with AI governance rules.

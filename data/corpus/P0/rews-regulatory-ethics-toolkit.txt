Purpose of the Regulatory & Ethics Toolkit

The GA4GH Regulatory and Ethics Work Stream (REWS) develops practical guidance for responsible genomic and health-related data sharing across borders. The Regulatory & Ethics Toolkit helps researchers, biobanks, Data Access Committees (DACs), and institutions align study consent, data-use language, and governance with international norms and jurisdiction-specific requirements.

Toolkit components relevant to RegBot navigation

1. Framework for Responsible Sharing (FRS) — foundational principles: respect for persons, privacy, data quality, transparency, accountability, and security. See P0 ga4gh-frs-en.pdf.

2. Consent Policy and Consent Toolkit — standard clauses for collection, use, sharing, withdrawal, and recontact. See P0 ga4gh-consent-policy.pdf and P1 MRCG/DUO brief.

3. Machine Readable Consent Guidance (MRCG) and Data Use Ontology (DUO) — map human-readable consent to machine-readable access codes for DAC review.

4. GDPR Forum Policy Briefs — EU-focused primers on consent, DPIA, transfers, and research processing (P1 corpus).

5. Regional Jurisdiction resources — East Asia legal basis index and per-country excerpts (P2 corpus: SG, CN, TW, KR, JP, HK).

How REWS guidance supports cross-border genomic sharing review

Researchers preparing international collaborations should:
- Identify source jurisdiction(s) for samples and data (see P2 regional excerpts).
- Compare consent / data-use language against GA4GH Consent Policy themes (purpose, sharing, secondary use, withdrawal, recontact).
- Check whether proposed use fits participant authorisation and DUO codes where datasets are coded.
- Document transfer mechanisms (contractual, adequacy, consent-based) for each destination country.
- Route ambiguous cases to institutional ethics review and, where applicable, DPO or DAC.

REWS relationship to institutional governance

REWS materials inform but do not replace:
- Institutional Review Boards / Research Ethics Committees
- Data Protection Officers and privacy impact assessments
- Data Access Committees and material/data transfer agreements
- National competent authorities (HGR approval in China, HBRA in Singapore, etc.)

RegBot review prompts (REWS-aligned)

- Does the study protocol cite applicable GA4GH framework principles (FRS pillars)?
- Is consent language consistent with GA4GH Consent Policy on international sharing and future research?
- Are DUO/consent-code restrictions respected for the requested data use?
- For multi-jurisdiction flows, has each leg (export, import, cloud processing) been mapped to a legal basis?
- Is there an escalation path when retrieval confidence is low (human review, not automated compliance)?

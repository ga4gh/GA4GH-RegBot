Policy purpose

The GA4GH Privacy and Security Policy sets expectations for organisations that generate, hold, or share genomic and health-related data within the GA4GH ecosystem. It complements the Framework for Responsible Sharing (FRS) by specifying privacy-by-design and security practices for federated and cloud-based research infrastructure.

Core privacy principles (summary)

- Lawful basis and transparency: processing must have a defined legal or ethical basis; data subjects or participants should receive clear information about collection, use, and sharing.
- Purpose limitation: data use should stay within purposes described at collection or approved by governance (ethics committee, DAC).
- Data minimisation: collect and retain only what is necessary for authorised research.
- Accuracy and quality: genomic pipelines should maintain data integrity and documented provenance.
- Storage limitation: retention schedules and secure deletion when no longer required.
- Accountability: designated roles for privacy, security, and access decisions.

Security expectations (summary)

- Access control: role-based access, authentication, and logging for sensitive genomic datasets.
- Encryption: protection in transit and at rest appropriate to sensitivity and threat model.
- Incident response: procedures for breach detection, notification, and remediation per applicable law.
- Third-party processors: due diligence and contracts (DPA/MTA/DUA) for cloud, sequencing vendors, and international collaborators.
- Re-identification risk: treat genomic data as potentially identifiable even when pseudonymised; apply controlled-access models.

Link to cross-border sharing

International transfers should combine GA4GH security baselines with jurisdiction-specific rules (GDPR Chapter V, PDPA transfer limitation, PIPL cross-border mechanisms, APPI foreign transfer consent, etc.). Security alone does not substitute for legal transfer basis.

Role of Data Access Committees

Data Access Committees (DACs) review requests to access controlled genomic and health datasets. They verify that the proposed research:
- Falls within participant consent and data-use limitations
- Has appropriate ethics approval where required
- Protects privacy and security of data subjects
- Serves legitimate scientific purposes with minimised re-identification risk

DAC review typically considers: research proposal summary, Data Use Ontology (DUO) permissions, institutional affiliation, publication plans, commercial use restrictions, and geographic limits.

GA4GH access principles

- Consent-aligned: access granted only for uses participants authorised (human-readable consent + DUO codes where available).
- Non-discriminatory: criteria applied consistently across applicants.
- Transparent: documented decisions and appeal pathways where institutions provide them.
- Time-bound: access may expire; ongoing use may require renewal or amendment.
- Attribution: researchers expected to acknowledge data sources and comply with publication embargoes if specified.

Data Use Ontology (DUO) in access decisions

DUO terms encode permissions and restrictions (e.g. general research use (GRU), health/medical/biomedical research (HMB), no commercial use (NPU), geographic restrictions). DACs match applicant intent to DUO tags attached to datasets. Mismatches require consent amendment, ethics review, or denial.

Cross-border access considerations

When applicants are in a different country from the data custodian:
- Confirm export/import compliance (HGR, PDPA, PIPL, GDPR transfers, etc.)
- Verify recipient institution security and subprocessor locations
- Check whether cloud analysis jurisdictions were disclosed to participants
- Ensure MTA/DUA covers international collaborators and downstream sharing

RegBot review prompts for consent / DUL vs DAC workflow

- Can each proposed use be mapped to a DUO code or consent clause?
- Does the application disclose all countries where analysis will occur?
- Are commercial, moratorium, or disease-specific restrictions reflected in the DUL?
- If access is for linkage or AI/secondary analysis, is broad/future-use consent plus ethics oversight documented?
